String Tambourine











String Instruments

String Instruments May Be Played With Or Without The Bow.
These instruments make a sound when struck, plucked, strummed or slapped
. Under The Sachs-Hornbostel system, these instruments are classified as chordophones.






What Is A String Tambourine

The string tambourine is suited to accompanying songs with chords, similar to a guitar.
And yet it is much simpler to play because you only have to hit the chords and don’t have to change the finger.





Where Was It Originated?

The string tambourine originally comes from France, where it was used in the Middle Ages
as an accompaniment instrument, similar to a hammered dulcimer or a chorded zither.
Each chord is made up of three strings which are hit with a mallet, a plectrum or the fingertips.





Can the pitch be changed and how?

Beating a tambourine harder makes the vibrations larger and so increases the volume of the sound.
However, it does not change the number or speed of the vibrations, so the pitch stays the same.
